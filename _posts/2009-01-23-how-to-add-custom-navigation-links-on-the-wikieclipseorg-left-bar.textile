title: How to add custom navigation links on the wiki.eclipse.org left bar 
---

This proved to be well hidden ; you can add your own links to the left bar of the wiki.eclipse.org website by adding them individually to the project metadata.

<img src="http://www.lunar-ocean.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/picture-24.png" alt="leftnavbar-project-metadata" title="leftnavbar-project-metadata" width="321" height="151" class="alignright size-full wp-image-228" />
<ol>
<li>
Log into portal.eclipse.org with your committer credentials
</li>
<li>
Go to your project, and click on "Maintain project meta-data"
</li>
<li>
Click to edit the "project left nav" property
</li>
<li>
Now you can enter your link information:
</li>
<li>
Just save it, and do it as often as needed.
</li>
<li>
Go to your <a href="http://wiki.eclipse.org/STP/BPMN_Component">wiki page</a> and edit it
</li>
<li>
Add this to the page: {% raw %}{{#eclipseproject:your.project.id}}{% endraw %}
I prefer <a href="http://wiki.eclipse.org/STP/BPMN_Component/Wiki_guidelines">using a template</a> for this though.
</li>
</ol>
